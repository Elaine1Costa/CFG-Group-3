MOVE!!!

If you had ever thought that physical activity is not for you, you may be surprised to know that:

  • Exercise boosts your mental wellbeing;
  • You just need 2h30min of moderate intensity activity per week, it includes fast walking and cycling;
  • You'll fell more energised and refreshed, with better performance in your studies.

    • So, what are you waiting for!? Exercise not only your brain (studying), take time to shake your body and you won't regret!

    • Take the most of your University sport facilities;
    • Ask your student union for information about free activities and sport clubs in your Uni;
    • Walk instead of getting the bus;
    • Take the stairs rather than the lift;
    • Ride a bike;
    • Download App which track your activity;
    • Go for a job along the day;
    • Kick a football around with your friends or have a game of volleyball at your free time;
    • During exams period take small regular breaks - 15min brisk walk to the library or around campus;
    • Try a gentle jog or doing some stretching at first in the morning;
    • Keep yourself motivated with the available Apps to track your physical activity!

      • Font: NHS - http://www.nhs.uk/Livewell/studenthealth/Pages/Exerciseandstudy.aspx

      And no matter how busy you are, even for a date you can choose to exercise!
